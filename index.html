I am a researcher at Amazon. I belong to the Core Machine Learning team. My research interests lie in the areas of machine learning and optimization. I am based in Bengaluru, India.

I completed my B.Tech in computer science from IIT Bombay in 2009. After a brief stint as a software engineer at Cisco, I returned to IIT Bombay as a grad student. I completed my Ph.D. under the supervision of Prof. J. Saketha Nath. I then worked as a postdoctoral researcher with Prof. Matthias Hein at Saarland University.

Research

My research interest primarily lies in machine learning and optimization. My doctoral work was focused on developing kernel learning frameworks for learning several problems jointly (a.k.a. multi-task learning). Key ideas include mixed-norm regularizations, saddle-point optimization approaches, and path-following algorithms. An interesting application I worked on was Rule Learning. We modeled this problem as a kernel learning problem. Below are some specific themes I have been working on recently.

Research at Amazon

  • Identifying product safety concerns: We develop algorithms that identify quality defects in products sold at Amazon's platform. The quality defects of interest to me are those that may result in safety concern.
  • Identify consumable products: The other problem I am working on is to identify products that are consumables.
