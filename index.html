I am a researcher at Amazon. I belong to the Core Machine Learning team. My research interests lie in the areas of machine learning and optimization. I am based in Bengaluru, India.

I completed my B.Tech in computer science from IIT Bombay in 2009. After a brief stint as a software engineer at Cisco, I returned to IIT Bombay as a grad student. I completed my Ph.D. under the supervision of Prof. J. Saketha Nath. I then worked as a postdoctoral researcher with Prof. Matthias Hein at Saarland University.

Research

My doctoral work was focused on developing kernel learning frameworks for learning several problems jointly. Key ideas include mixed-norm regularizations, saddle-point convex optimization approaches and path-following algorithms. An interesting application I worked on was Rule Learning. We modeled this problem as a kernel learning problem.

During post-doctoral research, I developed a projection-free convex approach for learning sparse positive semi-definite matrices. The proposed algorithm learned output kernel for multi-task learning problems very efficiently. It was implemented in the stochastic dual-coordinate ascent framework.

My research at Amazon is been on developing algorithms that identify quality defects in products sold at Amazon's platform. The quality defects of interest to me are those that may result in safety concern. The other problem I am working on is to identify products that are consumables.
